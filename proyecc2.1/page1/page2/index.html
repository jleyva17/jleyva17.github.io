The Aisles Control Process (ACP), which was pioneered in the late 20th century is a combination of processes used to subconsciously manipulate a population through the control of information flow. As Roger Aisles put it: people are lazy, they prefer the thinking be done for them. The goal here is to allow people access only to information that has been pre-digested and filtered for them. Effectively making it impossible for people to form a complete opinion. The main challenge in using the ACP is in knowing who your target is.
